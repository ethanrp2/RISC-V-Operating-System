Rohan and AZ were inseparable from the start, drawn together by a shared curiosity about the world and a knack for finding adventure in the smallest things. It didn’t matter if they were hiking a mountain or just picking up groceries—every moment with the two of them felt like an expedition, rich with laughter, debates, and an uncanny number of ridiculous, improvised challenges.
One chilly autumn afternoon, they decided to take a spontaneous road trip. Rohan, with his ever-analytical mind, had sketched out a route that would take them through the scenic backroads, peppered with hidden landmarks and eccentric diners he’d meticulously researched. AZ, on the other hand, was all for ditching the route. He had a firm belief that the best journeys required no map, just an instinctual pull to follow wherever felt right.
By hour two, Rohan’s carefully plotted course was abandoned. AZ had convinced him to turn down a barely visible dirt path, and before they knew it, they were winding their way through dense woods, a canopy of golden leaves filtering the sunlight into soft, dappled patches on the road ahead.
Eventually, they emerged into a clearing, where an old, forgotten farmhouse stood, ivy crawling up its sides and ancient oak trees surrounding it like loyal sentries. They stepped out of the car, the air thick with the earthy scent of fallen leaves and distant rain. To AZ, this was a dream—an unplanned treasure waiting to be explored.
Inside, the farmhouse was silent, save for the creak of the floorboards beneath their feet. Dust danced in the light streaming through cracked windows, and ancient furniture lay covered in sheets, frozen in time. Rohan noticed a book on a side table, its leather cover worn and delicate. He opened it, revealing a handwritten journal with entries from decades past. Each page contained stories of the family who had once lived there—of celebrations, hardships, and love.
As the day wore on, they got lost in the stories of the farmhouse, reading aloud, piecing together the lives of the people who had once called it home. They wondered aloud about the family’s descendants, about what had brought them here and why they’d left. It wasn’t the type of adventure Rohan had planned, but as they sat in that abandoned house, AZ and Rohan both knew they’d stumbled upon something meaningful.
By the time they returned to the car, the sun was setting, painting the sky in hues of pink and orange. AZ slapped Rohan on the back, grinning. “See? Who needs a map?”
Rohan just smiled, shaking his head. Maybe AZ was onto something after all.
